What is this

30 images. 4 questions.
A real conversation.

Uncover is a visual conversation tool. You look at 30 photographs — each one chosen to stir something — and pick the ones that resonate. That's it.

But here's what happens: the images you choose reveal patterns. Patterns about how you move through the world, what you're protecting, and what you need. It's not a quiz. There's no score. It's a mirror.

The idea behind it

We all have parts

Internal Family Systems (IFS) is a way of understanding the mind. The idea is simple: we all have different "parts" inside us. Some run the show. Some carry old pain. Some step in when things get hard. And underneath all of them is a calm, curious, compassionate core — your Self.

Most of the time, we only see the surface. Uncover helps you see the rest.

Part 1

Managers

The parts that run your daily life. They plan, achieve, control, and keep things together. They're the face you show the world.

Part 2

Exiles

The parts that carry old wounds — shame, fear, loneliness. They're often young, and they're kept hidden by your protectors.

Part 3

Firefighters

The parts that react when pain breaks through. They numb, escape, fight, or distract. They're not bad — they're desperate.

Part 4

Self

The calm center underneath all the parts. Curious, compassionate, clear. It's always there — sometimes just buried under protection.

How it works

Three steps, ten minutes

1

Browse

Look through 30 photographs. Don't analyze — just notice what catches your eye.

2

Choose

For each of 4 questions, pick the 3 images that resonate most. Trust your gut.

3

Reflect

See your picks mapped to your inner parts. Use the prompts to start a real conversation — with yourself or someone you trust.

Who this is for

Anyone ready to go below the surface

Uncover works for personal reflection, but it's especially powerful in conversation. It gives people a way to talk about inner experience without needing the vocabulary for it.

A Guide for Facilitators

This tool is informed by Internal Family Systems (IFS) — the idea that we all have different "parts" inside us. Some protect, some carry pain, some hold joy. These images help surface those parts without forcing anyone to name them directly.

01
Curiosity, not interpretation. Ask "what drew you to this?" — never "I think this means you're..." Let them lead.
02
Notice the protectors. If someone picks all "positive" images, gently notice: "I see a lot of strength here — is there anything these images are protecting?"
03
Question two is the heart. "What you carry but rarely show" surfaces exiled parts. Go slow. Sit with whatever comes up.
04
Share your own picks. Vulnerability invites vulnerability. When you go first with honesty, you give permission.
05
This is not therapy. It's a conversation starter. If something heavy surfaces: "Thank you for sharing that. Would you want to explore that more with someone?" Know your limits.
