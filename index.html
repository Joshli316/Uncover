A Guide for Facilitators

This tool is informed by Internal Family Systems (IFS) — the idea that we all have different "parts" inside us. Some protect, some carry pain, some hold joy. These images help surface those parts without forcing anyone to name them directly.

01
Curiosity, not interpretation. Ask "what drew you to this?" — never "I think this means you're..." Let them lead. Your job is to be curious, not clever.
02
Notice the protectors. If someone picks all "positive" images, gently notice that. "I see a lot of strength here — is there anything these images are protecting?"
03
Question two is the heart. "What you carry but rarely show" surfaces exiled parts. Go slow. Don't rush past emotion — sit with it.
04
Share your own picks. Vulnerability invites vulnerability. When you go first with honesty, you give permission for others to do the same.
05
This is not therapy. It's a conversation starter. If something heavy surfaces, honor it: "Thank you for sharing that. Would you want to explore that more with someone?" Know your limits.
